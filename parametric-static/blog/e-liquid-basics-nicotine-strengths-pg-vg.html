E‑liquid basics: nicotine strengths & PG/VG

What is e‑liquid? #

E‑liquid (a.k.a. vape juice) is the fluid vaporized by an e‑cigarette or vape device. When heated by the coil, it becomes an aerosol that carries flavor and, if present, nicotine. Most e‑liquids are a blend of two food/pharma‑grade base ingredients|propylene glycol (PG) and vegetable glycerin (VG)|plus flavorings and an optional nicotine solution. Understanding how PG, VG, and nicotine strength work together helps you dial in throat hit, smoothness, vapor production, and overall satisfaction.

PG vs VG: what’s the difference? #

Propylene Glycol (PG)

  • Texture: Thin and runny.
  • Flavor carry: Excellent|makes flavors feel sharp and defined.
  • Throat hit: Noticeable, similar to the bite from higher‑nic cigarettes.
  • Wicking: Flows easily through small cotton ports; ideal for low‑power pods and MTL (mouth‑to‑lung) tanks.

Vegetable Glycerin (VG)

  • Texture: Thick and syrupy.
  • Flavor carry: Softer, rounder taste; adds sweetness.
  • Vapor: Big, dense clouds|favored for DL (direct‑lung) vaping.
  • Wicking: Requires larger ports and more power to vaporize efficiently.

Neither PG nor VG is “better” in absolute terms. The right blend depends on your device, power level, and what you value|flavor sharpness and throat hit (more PG) versus smoothness and cloud volume (more VG).

Common PG/VG ratios #

  • 50/50: Balanced; great for small pods and MTL tanks. Good flavor and moderate throat hit with modest vapor.
  • 60/40 PG/VG: Extra throat hit and flavor clarity; wicks quickly in tight coils. Popular for tobacco/menthol profiles.
  • 70/30 VG/PG: Smoother and cloudier; common for sub‑ohm DL tanks and many dessert or fruit blends.
  • Max VG (80%+ VG): Ultra‑smooth, dense vapor for high‑power setups. May be too thick for small pods.

Nicotine strengths explained #

Nicotine strength is usually labeled in mg/mL (milligrams per milliliter). Some brands also show percentages. Quick conversion:

  • 1% ≈ 10 mg/mL
  • 2% ≈ 20 mg/mL
  • 3.5% ≈ 35 mg/mL

How much you need depends on the device and your prior nicotine intake:

  • Low (0|6 mg/mL): For high‑power DL tanks; smooth with big clouds. 0 mg is nicotine‑free.
  • Medium (9|12 mg/mL): For MTL tanks or restricted DL at moderate power.
  • High (18|24 mg/mL): For low‑power pods and classic MTL; pronounced throat hit.
  • Salt nic (20|50 mg/mL typical): Used in low‑power pods; smoother at high strengths (see below).

Remember: higher strength doesn’t always mean more satisfaction|too strong can feel harsh or dizzying. Match strength to wattage and airflow.

Freebase vs nicotine salts #

Freebase nicotine

  • Traditional form used for years in e‑liquids.
  • Stronger throat hit at a given mg/mL; great for lower strengths in powerful devices.
  • Common strengths for sub‑ohm: 0|6 mg/mL; for MTL: 6|18 mg/mL.

Nicotine salts

  • Nicotine combined with an acid (e.g., benzoic) to lower pH and smooth the hit.
  • Comfortable at higher strengths (20|50 mg/mL) in small, low‑watt devices.
  • Not intended for high‑power, high‑flow setups|can deliver too much nicotine too quickly.

Rule of thumb: Use freebase for big, airy DL rigs at low strengths; use salts for compact pods at higher strengths.

Choosing the right strength & ratio #

Use your device type and desired feel to select a starting point, then adjust.

Device style Coil / power Recommended nicotine Typical PG/VG Feel
Small pod (MTL) High‑ohm / low‑watt 20|35 mg/mL salt (or 12|18 mg freebase) 50/50 to 60/40 PG/VG Defined flavor, noticeable hit, modest vapor
MTL tank (replaceable coils) Mid‑ohm / 10|20 W 9|18 mg/mL freebase (or 10|20 mg salt) 50/50 to 60/40 PG/VG Balanced hit and flavor
Sub‑ohm DL tank Low‑ohm / 30|80 W+ 0|6 mg/mL freebase 70/30 VG/PG to Max VG Smooth, dense clouds
RDL (restricted DL) / AIO 0.3|0.8 Ω / 20|40 W 3|9 mg/mL freebase 60/40 to 70/30 VG/PG Good flavor, moderate clouds

Tune from there: If it’s harsh, lower nicotine or increase VG. If satisfaction is lacking, raise nicotine slightly or use a tighter airflow device with more PG.

Safety, storage & handling #

  • Keep out of reach of children and pets. Nicotine can be toxic if ingested or absorbed in high amounts.
  • Store cool, dark, and upright. Heat and light degrade nicotine and flavors. Close caps tightly.
  • Skin contact: Wipe spills immediately and wash with soap and water.
  • Allergies/sensitivities: If PG causes irritation, try higher‑VG blends. Discontinue if symptoms persist.
  • Local laws: Follow age restrictions and regulations in your area.

Quick reference #

  • More PG = thinner, sharper flavor, stronger throat hit.
  • More VG = thicker, smoother, bigger clouds.
  • Pods/MTL → 50/50 base, 12|35 mg (often salts).
  • Sub‑ohm DL → 70/30 VG/PG, 0|6 mg (freebase).
  • If harsh → lower nicotine or raise VG.
  • If weak → raise nicotine slightly or tighten airflow.
