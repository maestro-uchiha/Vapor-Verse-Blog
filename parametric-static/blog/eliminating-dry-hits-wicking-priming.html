Eliminating dry hits: wicking & priming

What this guide covers #

Dry hits happen when the coil outpaces the liquid supply to the wick. The result is a scorched, acrid puff that can ruin a coil and your mood. This guide shows you how to stop them|by dialing in wicking, priming, power, airflow, and liquid choices across pods, sub‑ohm tanks, and rebuildables.

What is a dry hit? #

  • Symptoms: Burnt or papery taste, throat sting, sudden heat spike, little vapor.
  • Why it happens: The wick surface near the coil dries out; with no liquid to absorb heat, fiber and residue scorch.
  • Early warning signs: Bubbling stops after a pull, coil sounds “crispy,” flavor thins, or you see no air bubbles rise in the tank after puffs.

The dry‑hit triangle #

Eliminate dry hits by balancing three variables:

  • Liquid flow: PG/VG ratio, temperature, bottle age, and sweetener level affect viscosity and wicking.
  • Wick pathway: Cotton density, how tails sit in juice ports, and deck airflow all govern replenishment.
  • Heat demand: Wattage/voltage, coil surface area, puff length, and airflow set evaporation rate.

Wicking & cotton fundamentals #

  • Pick suitable cotton: Use clean, uniform fibers (pads or laces). Avoid compressed makeup cotton with unknown additives.
  • Right snugness: The wick should slide through the coil with gentle resistance|pulling the mod shouldn’t move. Too tight chokes capillary flow; too loose causes hot spots.
  • Comb & trim: Lightly comb tails to remove bulk, then trim to just reach the deck/ports. Fluff ends; do not stuff.
    • RTA juice wells: Fill the ledge, not the hole. You’re creating a dam, not a cork.
    • RDTA: Tails should just kiss the reservoir|long whiskers slow return flow.
  • Shape the shoulders: Cut a subtle angle so the top of the tail is thinner than the bottom; this prevents choking at the port entrance.
  • Saturate the core: Pre‑wet the coil area first; massage liquid in until fibers turn translucent.
  • Check airflow alignment: For side/bottom airflow decks, center the coil so air hits the coil’s midline, not the cotton.

Priming, step by step #

Device How to prime First‑use settings
Pre‑made coil (sub‑ohm tank) Add a few drops onto visible cotton openings. Assemble, fill, and wait 5|10 minutes. Tilt/roll to release trapped bubbles. Start at the bottom of the recommended watt range; take 3|5 short puffs, then step up gradually.
Pod/Cartridge Fill slowly; stop before the fill line. Let sit 5|10 minutes. Take a few gentle primer pulls without firing. If adjustable, start with lower power/airflow mid‑closed; open as flavor stabilizes.
RTA/RDTA After wicking, paint liquid on the coil and tails until saturated. Fill tank, wait a few minutes, and watch for steady bubble release after the first pulls. Pulse at low wattage to evaporate excess on the surface, then raise slowly while monitoring flavor/temperature.

Rule of thumb: If you don’t see small bubbles rising after a puff, the wick may be too tight or ports blocked|re‑wick before you scorch it.

Power, airflow & puff style #

  • Match power to coil & liquid: High‑VG and sweet liquids need more airflow and slightly lower wattage to avoid caramelizing the wick.
  • Puff pacing: Chain vaping? Add a few seconds between pulls for the wick to refill, or drop power a notch.
  • Airflow balance: Too tight at high power superheats the coil face; crack airflow open until heat feels even through the pull.
  • Temperature control (if available): Use SS/Ni/Ti coils as supported; set a comfortable temp ceiling to prevent overheat during long draws.
  • Puff length: Long, forceful pulls can outstrip capillary action. Shorten slightly or open airflow.

E‑liquid viscosity & additives #

  • PG/VG basics: 50/50 wicks easily in small pods/MTL; 60/40|70/30 suits mesh sub‑ohm tanks and airy RDL/DTL.
  • Temperature swing: Liquids thicken in cold rooms/cars|expect slower wicking. Warm bottles in your hands before filling.
  • Sweeteners: Heavily sweetened desserts caramelize on coils; reduce wattage or alternate with cleaner liquids to extend life.
  • Age & oxidation: Very old or darkened liquid can taste harsh and wick poorly. When in doubt, replace.

Device‑specific tips #

  • Small MTL pods: Prefer 50/50 liquids and 0.8|1.2Ω coils; avoid aggressive chain puffs. Replace pods when flavor mutes or draw tightens.
  • Sub‑ohm tanks: Watch bubble behavior after each puff. If bubbles stall, slightly open airflow or lower wattage.
  • RTAs/RDTAs: If you must force tails into ports, they’re too thick. Thin and re‑seat so they just rest at the entrance.
  • DIY coil builders: Space wraps or lightly strum hotspots before wicking; uneven heat creates localized burning even with good cotton.

Troubleshooting (quick table) #

Symptom Likely cause Fix
Burnt taste on first use Under‑primed wick Refill, wait 10 min, take primer pulls; start at low wattage
Harsh after 2|3 puffs Wattage too high / airflow too tight Lower power, open airflow slightly, shorten draws
No bubbles after pulls Wick too dense / ports blocked Re‑wick with thinner tails; comb and trim
Flavor fades then burns Caramelized sweeteners on coil Reduce power, switch to cleaner liquid, replace coil
Okay at home, bad in cold Liquid too thick when chilled Use more PG or warm device/liquid before use

Myths to ignore #

  • “More cotton stops leaks.” Over‑stuffing blocks flow and causes dry hits. Seal with shape, not bulk.
  • “Just crank wattage to ‘break in.’” High heat on a fresh wick scorches fibers. Start low; step up slowly.
  • “Poke extra holes in pods.” You’ll compromise vacuum and flood the coil. Use appropriate liquid and replace worn pods.

At‑a‑glance checklist #

  • Wick slides snugly; tails thinned and placed, not stuffed.
  • Coil fully saturated; wait 5|10 minutes after filling.
  • Begin at low wattage; raise in small steps while monitoring heat/flavor.
  • Watch for post‑puff bubbles; adjust if they stop appearing.
  • Match PG/VG to device; favor cleaner liquids for coil longevity.
  • Add a few seconds between pulls, especially on small pods.

FAQs #

Do I need to change cotton every time I change flavors?

For rebuildables, yes if flavors clash or sweetness is high; otherwise a gentle rinse and dry burn (for wire‑only, not cotton) before re‑wicking works. For pods/coils, a few fills of a similar profile is fine, but expect some carryover.

Is priming different for mesh vs round‑wire?

Mesh has more surface area and wicks aggressively|prime thoroughly and start even lower in the range. Round wire is more forgiving but still needs full saturation.

Can temperature control fully prevent dry hits?

It reduces risk with compatible wires, but poor wicking can still outpace liquid flow. TC is a safety net, not a cure‑all.
