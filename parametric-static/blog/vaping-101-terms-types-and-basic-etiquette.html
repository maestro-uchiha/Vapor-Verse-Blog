Vaping 101: Terms, types, and basic etiquette

Adult-only note: Vaping products contain nicotine, which is addictive and not risk‑free. Follow local laws, keep devices and liquids away from children and pets, and speak with a healthcare professional if you have questions about nicotine use.

What isvaping? #

Vaping is the use of an electronic device to heat a liquid (often containing nicotine and flavorings) into an aerosol you inhale. Unlike burning tobacco, there’s no combustion. A typical setup has a battery, a chipset that regulates power, a tank or pod that holds e‑liquid, a wick that draws liquid to a coil, and a mouthpiece.

When you press the fire button (or take an auto‑draw puff), the coil heats up. Liquid on the wick vaporizes and you inhale the aerosol through the mouthpiece. Two broad styles exist:

  • Mouth‑to‑Lung (MTL): A tighter draw that mimics cigarettes, lower vapor, usually higher nicotine.
  • Direct‑to‑Lung (DTL): An airier draw straight to the lungs, bigger clouds, usually lower nicotine.

Common terms (quick glossary) #

Ohm (Ω)
Resistance of the coil. High‑ohm (≥1.0 Ω) suits MTL; low‑ohm (<0.8 Ω) suits DTL.
Watt (W)
Power delivered to the coil. More watts = warmer vapor (within safe limits).
mAh
Battery capacity. Higher mAh = potentially longer time between charges.
Pod
Small tank + coil unit. Can be prefilled or refillable.
Coil/Head
Replaceable heating element (often cotton wick + wire/mesh).
PG/VG
Propylene glycol (throat hit/flavor) and vegetable glycerin (smoothness/vapor).
Nicotine salt
Nicotine formulated for smoother high‑strength use at low power.
Freebase nicotine
Traditional form used at lower strengths for mid/high power devices.
Dry hit
Burnt taste from insufficient liquid on the wick.
Flooding/Gurgle
Too much liquid in the coil or chimney.

Device types #

There’s a device for nearly every preference. This overview can help you narrow choices.

E‑liquid basics #

Ingredients: Most e‑liquids blend PG, VG, flavorings, and optionally nicotine. Ratios matter:

  • 50/50 PG/VG: Balanced; great for pods and MTL.
  • 60|70% VG: Smoother, more vapor; mid‑power devices.
  • 70|80% VG: Dense vapor for sub‑ohm tanks; needs big wicking ports.

Nicotine types & strengths:

  • Nicotine salt is smoother at higher strengths (e.g., 10|20 mg/mL and up) and suits low‑power pods.
  • Freebase nicotine is common at 3|12 mg/mL for mid/high power use.

Choosing a starting strength (general guidance):

If you previously smoked… Typical device Try around
Occasionally / very light Open/closed pod (low power) 3|6 mg freebase or 5|10 mg salt
Light|moderate daily Open pod or pen 6|12 mg freebase or 10|20 mg salt
Heavy daily Closed pod or refillable pod (low power) 12 mg freebase or 18|20+ mg salt

Everyone’s sensitivity differs. If you feel dizzy, nauseous, or headachy, reduce nicotine or take longer breaks. If you feel unsatisfied, the opposite may be true. When in doubt, consult a clinician.

Storage: Keep bottles upright, tightly closed, away from heat/light; lock away from children and pets.

Getting started safely #

  1. Buy responsibly. Use reputable retailers; avoid counterfeit-looking hardware or liquids.
  2. Charge with care. Use the cable provided or a quality, low‑amp charger. Don’t charge unattended or overnight.
  3. Prime new coils/pods. Add a few drops of liquid to exposed cotton (if applicable), fill the tank/pod, and let it sit 5|10 minutes before first puffs.
  4. Start low, adjust up. Begin at the bottom of the coil’s recommended wattage and increase slowly to taste.
  5. Mind airflow. Tighter for MTL; more open for DTL. Adjust in small steps.
  6. Hydrate. PG can feel drying; drink water.

Basic vaping etiquette #

  • Ask first. Don’t assume vaping is welcome, even outdoors in tight spaces.
  • Respect posted rules. Many venues treat vaping like smoking. If in doubt, step outside to a designated area.
  • Be mindful of others. Keep distance, blow vapor away from people, doorways, and open windows. Avoid clouds in crowds.
  • Zero around kids and pets. Never vape in cars or rooms with children; store liquids securely and clean any spills immediately.
  • Keep scents subtle. Strong flavors can bother others|choose neutral profiles in shared spaces.
  • Dispose responsibly. Batteries and e‑waste require proper recycling; never litter pods or disposables.

Maintenance & troubleshooting #

Routine care: Wipe condensation from contacts, keep the mouthpiece clean, and check for cracks or loose seals. Replace coils/pods when flavor dulls or tastes burnt.

Common issues:

  • Burnt taste (dry hit): Lower wattage, slow your puffing, ensure the pod/tank is at least half full, and let the coil re‑saturate.
  • Leaking: Make sure seals are seated, keep the device upright, avoid extreme heat, and replace worn pods/coils.
  • Gurgling/flooding: Flick excess liquid out (away from people), take a few primer puffs without firing, then resume at lower power.
  • Poor battery life: Reduce wattage, close airflow slightly, carry a spare pod/coil, or choose a higher‑mAh device.

Battery safety (important): Don’t carry loose batteries with keys/coins. Use intact wraps; replace damaged cells. Only use compatible chargers and keep devices out of direct sun.

Travel & local rules #

  • Check laws before you go. Rules differ by region|some restrict flavors, nicotine strengths, or disposables, and many ban indoor use.
  • Flying: Devices and batteries go in carry‑on (not checked). Empty tanks to prevent leaks. No vaping on planes or in bathrooms.
  • Public spaces: Treat vaping like smoking unless signage says otherwise.
